# Common Mistakes to Avoid

Learn from others' errors. These are the most frequent mistakes predictors make—and how to avoid them.

## The Big Five

### 1. Chasing Losses

<div className="p-5 rounded-xl bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 mt-4">
  <h4 className="font-bold m-0 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">The Pattern</h4>
  <p className="mt-2">Lose a bet → Bet bigger to "get it back" → Lose again → Repeat until broke</p>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">Why It Happens</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Emotional response to loss</li>
    <li>Desire to "break even" for the day</li>
    <li>Gambling mentality taking over</li>
  </ul>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-green-800 dark:text-green-200">The Fix</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Set a daily loss limit (e.g., stop after 3 losses)</li>
    <li>Take a break after any loss</li>
    <li>Treat each bet independently</li>
    <li>Accept that losses are part of the game</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### 2. Overtrading

<div className="p-5 rounded-xl bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 mt-4">
  <h4 className="font-bold m-0 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">The Pattern</h4>
  <p className="mt-2">Betting on everything → Win rate drops → Frustrated → Bet even more</p>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">Why It Happens</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Boredom between "good" opportunities</li>
    <li>Fear of missing out (FOMO)</li>
    <li>Treating prediction as entertainment</li>
  </ul>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-green-800 dark:text-green-200">The Fix</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Set a maximum bets per day limit</li>
    <li>Only bet when you have clear conviction</li>
    <li>Have strict entry criteria</li>
    <li>Occupy yourself between bets</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### 3. No Risk Management

<div className="p-5 rounded-xl bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 mt-4">
  <h4 className="font-bold m-0 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">The Pattern</h4>
  <p className="mt-2">Bet 50% of funds on "sure thing" → Lose → Devastated → Can't recover</p>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">Why It Happens</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Overconfidence in a single prediction</li>
    <li>Not understanding variance</li>
    <li>Getting greedy</li>
  </ul>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-green-800 dark:text-green-200">The Fix</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Never bet more than 2-5% on one prediction</li>
    <li>Use consistent bet sizing</li>
    <li>Understand that even 80% favorites lose 20% of the time</li>
    <li>Protect your bankroll above all</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### 4. Confirmation Bias

<div className="p-5 rounded-xl bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 mt-4">
  <h4 className="font-bold m-0 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">The Pattern</h4>
  <p className="mt-2">Form opinion → Only seek supporting evidence → Ignore warning signs → Lose</p>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">Why It Happens</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Natural human psychology</li>
    <li>Ego tied to being "right"</li>
    <li>Echo chambers and bias bubbles</li>
  </ul>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-green-800 dark:text-green-200">The Fix</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Actively search for opposing views</li>
    <li>Ask "Why might I be wrong?"</li>
    <li>Assign probability, not certainty</li>
    <li>Welcome information that challenges you</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### 5. Emotional Decision-Making

<div className="p-5 rounded-xl bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 mt-4">
  <h4 className="font-bold m-0 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">The Pattern</h4>
  <p className="mt-2">Feel strongly about something → Bet based on feelings → Reality differs → Lose</p>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-red-800 dark:text-red-200">Why It Happens</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Betting on favorite team/outcome</li>
    <li>Anger, excitement, or fear</li>
    <li>Wishful thinking</li>
  </ul>

  <h4 className="font-bold mt-4 text-green-800 dark:text-green-200">The Fix</h4>
  <ul className="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Never bet when emotional</li>
    <li>Avoid betting on teams/outcomes you're attached to</li>
    <li>Use data, not feelings</li>
    <li>Create rules and follow them</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## TruthScore-Specific Mistakes

### Mistake: Volume Without Accuracy

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-4">
  **The issue:** Thinking more trades = higher score

  **Reality:** With < 50% win rate, more volume LOWERS your score

  **The fix:** Stop trading, fix your strategy, then return
</div>

### Mistake: Impatience with the System

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-4">
  **The issue:** Frustrated by 14-day cap, experience scaling, etc.

  **Reality:** These protections make rankings meaningful

  **The fix:** Use waiting time to improve skills, not to get frustrated
</div>

### Mistake: Cherry-Picking Imports

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-4">
  **The issue:** Trying to only import winning trades

  **Reality:** All trades are imported - this isn't possible

  **The fix:** Accept your full record and focus on improving it
</div>

## The Mistake Cycle

Most traders go through this cycle:

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-gray-50 dark:bg-gray-800 mt-4 text-center">
  <p className="m-0">
    Excitement → Overconfidence → Big loss → Desperation → Bigger loss → Reality check → Learning
  </p>
</div>

<Callout type="info">
  **The goal:** Short-circuit this cycle by learning from others' mistakes instead of your own.
</Callout>

## Self-Assessment Checklist

Be honest with yourself:

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-gray-50 dark:bg-gray-800 mt-4">
  **Ask yourself:**

  - [ ] Do I have a clear strategy, or am I winging it?
  - [ ] Do I track all my bets?
  - [ ] Do I have a stop-loss (daily/weekly)?
  - [ ] Can I walk away after a loss?
  - [ ] Do I size bets consistently?
  - [ ] Am I betting with money I can lose?
  - [ ] Do I research before betting?
  - [ ] Can I bet against my preferences?
  - [ ] Do I learn from losses?
  - [ ] Am I patient?

  **Scoring:**
  - 9-10 checks: Excellent foundation
  - 6-8 checks: Good, room to improve
  - 3-5 checks: Significant risk
  - 0-2 checks: Stop trading until you fix this
</div>
