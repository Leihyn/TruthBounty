# Beginner's Guide to Predictions

New to prediction markets? This guide will set you up for success with foundational concepts, safe practices, and realistic expectations.

## What Are Prediction Markets?

<div className="p-6 rounded-xl bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 mt-6">
  **Prediction markets** are platforms where you bet on the outcomes of events. If you're right, you profit. If you're wrong, you lose your stake.

  Unlike gambling, prediction markets reward **skill and knowledge**. Good predictors consistently outperform random chance.
</div>

## Building Good Habits

### Keep a Trading Journal

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-gray-50 dark:bg-gray-800 mt-4">
  For each prediction, record:
  - Date and time
  - Platform
  - What you bet on
  - Why you made this bet

  - Result
  - What you learned

Every week, look back:

- What mistakes did you make?

## Understanding Odds

### Probability vs. Odds

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 mt-4">
  **Probability** = Chance of something happening (0-100%)
  **Odds** = How much you win relative to stake

  | Probability | Decimal Odds | If You Bet $10 |
  |-------------|--------------|----------------|
  | 90% likely | 1.11 | Win $11 (profit $1) |
  | 50% likely | 2.00 | Win $20 (profit $10) |
  | 20% likely | 5.00 | Win $50 (profit $40) |
</div>

### Finding Value

Value exists when you think something is MORE likely than the odds suggest:

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/20 mt-4">
  **Example:**
  - Market says: 30% chance (odds 3.33)
  - You believe: 50% chance
  - This is a "value bet" - the expected return is positive

  Over many bets, value bets profit even if individual bets lose.
</div>

## Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Betting on Everything

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-2">
  **Problem:** Treating prediction markets like entertainment betting on everything.

  **Solution:** Only bet when you genuinely believe you have an edge. Quality over quantity.
</div>

### 2. Ignoring Bankroll Management

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-2">
  **Problem:** Betting 50% of your funds on one "sure thing."

  **Solution:** Never bet more than 2-5% of your total bankroll on a single prediction.
</div>

### 3. Confirmation Bias

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-2">
  **Problem:** Only seeing evidence that supports your bet, ignoring contrary evidence.

  **Solution:** Actively look for reasons you might be wrong before betting.
</div>

### 4. Emotional Trading

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-2">
  **Problem:** Betting when angry, excited, drunk, or after losses.

  **Solution:** Make rules for yourself. Step away when emotional.
</div>

### 5. No Record Keeping

<div className="p-4 rounded-xl bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 mt-2">
  **Problem:** Not tracking bets, so you can't learn from patterns.

  **Solution:** Keep a simple spreadsheet or journal. Review regularly.
</div>

## Realistic Expectations
